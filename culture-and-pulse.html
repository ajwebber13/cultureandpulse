Black History Drops
1865
Juneteenth: The Day Freedom Finally Arrived

On June 19, 1865 — two and a half years after the Emancipation Proclamation — Union soldiers arrived in Galveston, TX and announced that enslaved people were free. The news had been deliberately withheld by enslavers to keep Black labor captive.

General Gordon Granger
1908
Jack Johnson: The First Black Heavyweight Champion of the World

Jack Johnson's 1908 victory over Tommy Burns made him the first Black heavyweight boxing champion. His dominance triggered a nationwide search for a "Great White Hope" — and permanently altered the politics of American sport.

Jack Johnson
1947
Jackie Robinson Broke Baseball's Color Line — and the Nation Watched

When Jackie Robinson stepped onto Ebbets Field on April 15, 1947, he did more than play baseball. He forced America to confront what it claimed to stand for — and changed every sport that came after him.

Jackie Robinson

Culture & Pulse was born out of a simple truth: the stories that shape Black and Brown communities are too often told by people outside of them.

I built this platform because I've lived at the intersection of sports, history, and culture my entire life. Growing up in New Orleans, rooted in an AME Zion legacy that goes back four generations, I understand what it means when the media gets our story wrong — and what it feels like when someone finally gets it right.

Culture & Pulse is that correction. Sports × Black History × Culture — not as a niche, but as the main event.
